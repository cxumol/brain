Also called the Noether current, is a current $j_N^\mu$ that satisfies the equation:

$$\partial_\mu J_N^\mu = 0$$

From Wikipedia:

Indeed, integrating the continuity equation over a volume $V$, large enough to have no net currents through its surface, leads to the conservation law:

$$\frac{\partial}{\partial t}Q = 0,$$

where $Q = \int_V j^0 dV$ is the conserved quantity
