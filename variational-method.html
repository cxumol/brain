brain/Variational method

The variational method is a method in quantum mechanics used to approximate the ground state of a system.

The method revolves around choosing an initial "trial wave function" that depends on one or more parameter(s) (here denoted $\alpha$). The value of $\alpha$ that minimizes the resulting expected energy is then used to yield an approximation of the true ground state wave function. The expectation value of the energy also serves as an upper bound to the ground state energy.
